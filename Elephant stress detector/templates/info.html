Semi-Tamed Behavior & Pinnawala Context

The elephants at Pinnawala are "semi-tamed," meaning they are accustomed to human presence and daily routines (bathing at Ma Oya river, feeding hours) but retain wild instincts.

Specific Stressors: Unlike wild herds, Pinnawala elephants may experience stress from:

  • Restricted movement during non-bathing hours.
  • Dominance hierarchies within the orphan group.
  • High visitor density.

This AI model is calibrated to detect the stereotypic swaying that specifically arises when these intelligent animals are waiting or confined.
